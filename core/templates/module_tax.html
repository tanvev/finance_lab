Income Tax: The Indian Budgeting Boss Guide

Understand how income tax works in India, how deductions reduce your tax, and which tax regime is best for you—because giving the government less is the ultimate power move.

What You Will Learn

Taxes might look like ancient Sanskrit written on a scroll, but we’re here to decode the simple rules. We'll arm you with the knowledge on deductions, HRA, capital gains, and more. Think of this as unlocking your financial cheat codes—and unlike your last dating phase, this one is worth the effort.

What Even *Is* Income Tax?

Income tax is the mandatory annual subscription fee that keeps the country running. It funds everything from the roads you complain about on Twitter to the UPI payment system that actually works. It's basically paying for India’s operating system—and yes, you have to reboot it every year.

5 Heads of Income:

  • Salary : Your regular paycheck. The one your employer already took tax from (TDS). Thanks, I hate it.
  • House Property : Rental income. If you own one, congratulations, you're that person.
  • Capital Gains : The profit (or loss) from selling assets like stocks or that overpriced flat.
  • Business / Profession : Earnings from your side-hustle or main-hustle. The government always knows.
  • Other Sources : The financial leftovers—like interest from your "emergency" FD or that time you actually won the lottery. (Don't forget to report this, seriously.)

Deductions like 80C/80D reduce taxable income — basically discount codes.They allow you to reduce your Taxable Income—meaning you pay tax on a lower amount. It’s the ultimate jugaad (hack) but it's 100% legal.

Old vs New Tax Regime

Old = customise with deductions • New = simpler with lower slabs.

✨ Old Regime

  • High effort, high reward. The one your CA loves. Requires actual planning and receipts.
  • Includes HRA, the full ₹1.5L from 80C, 80D (Health Insurance), and the home loan interest benefit.
  • Hard Mode. You must actively opt for this regime and gather all the receipts.

⚡ New Regime

  • Low effort, low reward (for most). The government’s default choice. Zero brain cells required.
  • Lower tax slabs (technically, you pay less at certain income points). Also gets a Standard Deduction
  • Easy Mode. The automatic default. File and forget (mostly)

HRA (House Rent Allowance)

If you're still not that person who owns property, this is your small victory. HRA allows a reduction in your taxable salary. The government checks your math and only exempts the minimum of these three:

  1. Actual HRA received (The number your HR sent)
  2. Rent paid MINUS 10% of (Basic Salary + DA) (The actual formula that makes you feel financially illiterate)
  3. 50% of (Basic Salary + DA) (Metro cities: Mumbai, Delhi, Chennai, Kolkata) / 40% (Non-metro cities)

If your annual rent is over ₹1 Lakh, you must provide your landlord's PAN. If they don't have one? Get a legal declaration. Don't let your landlord's lack of compliance ruin your tax game.

Capital Gains: The Asset Sale Tax

This is the tax on profit from selling assets. If you trade options, congratulations, you probably have a Business/Profession income, not a simple Capital Gain.

  • Short Term Capital Gains (STCG):
    • Asset held for less than 12 months.
    • Flat 15% tax. The tax equivalent of impulse buying.
  • Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG):
    • Asset held for more than 12 months.
    • Taxed at a kinder 10% only on gains exceeding ₹1,00,000. The reward for having patience.

Common Mistakes Beginners Make

  • Not reporting savings interest : The most common rookie mistake. You report the income first, then claim the 80TTA deduction (up to ₹10,000).
  • Using the Wrong ITR Form : Did you sell a share? You probably can't use ITR-1. Did you run a business? You need ITR-3 or ITR-4 (Sugam). Pick wisely.
  • Mismatching TDS with 26AS/AIS : Your employer said they deducted ₹X, but 26AS says ₹Y. Trust 26AS. Get the mismatch corrected by the deductor (your employer/bank) before filing..
  • Not Verifying the ITR : Filing is only half the battle. You must e-Verify your return (via Aadhaar OTP, Net Banking, etc.) within 30 days, or your return is considered never filed. It’s a literal "click this button or it never happened" scenario.
